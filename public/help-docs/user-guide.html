1. What the portal is for

The Guarantee Referral Portal lets partner staff refer tenants who cannot pass standard referencing to opndoor's professional guarantor service, and track each referral from Sent to Paid to Deed Issued.

opndoor provides a Deed of Guarantee in favour of the property, for tenants who cannot provide their own guarantor. opndoor is a professional guarantor service — not insurance — and is not a party to, or named on, the tenancy agreement. The referring agent remains the claim contact.

2. Signing in with two-factor

The portal protects live tenant and commercial data, so every account signs in with two-factor authentication (2FA).

  1. Enter your work email and password.
  2. At the second step, enter the 6-digit code from your authenticator app.
  3. The first time you sign in you will be prompted to enrol your authenticator by scanning a QR code — keep that app; you'll use it every sign-in.

Lost your authenticator? Ask an opndoor admin to reset it. They can clear your enrolment so you set it up again at your next sign-in.

3. Sending a referral

Open New application and complete the sections in order:

  1. Tenant — name, contact details and date of birth.
  2. Property — the address being let.
  3. Tenancy — the rent and the tenancy start date.
  4. Agent & branch — search for an existing agency and branch, or add a new one on the fly if it isn't listed.

Submit to send the referral to the tenant. You do not enter a guarantee reference, issue date or expiry — those are assigned automatically once the guarantee is issued and appear on the application's detail view.

Added an agency or branch that wasn't listed? It appears on the Agencies & branches screen and is reviewed by opndoor for possible duplicates before it becomes a canonical record. Your referral is attached to it either way.

4. Sent, Paid, Deed Issued

Every referral moves through the same lifecycle. These are the statuses you'll see across the dashboard and Applications screen:

StatusWhat it means
SentThe referral has been sent to the tenant to pay the guarantor fee.
PaidThe guarantor fee — one month's rent — has been paid.
Deed IssuedThe Deed of Guarantee has been issued and stored against the record.
WithdrawnThe referral was withdrawn before completing.
RefundedA paid fee was refunded (for example, the let fell through).

5. The guarantee deed

The Deed of Guarantee is the legal instrument opndoor issues in favour of the property. It gives the landlord the reassurance a personal guarantor would, standing behind the tenant's obligations under the tenancy.

  • opndoor is not a party to the tenancy agreement and is not named on it.
  • The referring agent is the claim contact — anything arising under the guarantee goes through them.
  • The deed is stored against the application once issued; you can open it from the application detail view.

Changing the tenancy start date: if a tenancy date needs to move, it can be amended as long as the new date is within 7 days of the payment date. Amending it reissues the deed with the corrected date. This is available to opndoor admins and management on the detail view.

6. Tracking & the dashboard

The dashboard is your live view of performance:

  • The Sent → Paid → Deed Issued funnel shows conversion at each stage.
  • The time period selector at the top — from the last 7 days through to all time — reframes every figure and chart on the page.
  • Volume charts show referrals over the selected period. Breakdown tables are labelled within the selected period where they are period-clipped.
  • Export downloads the analytics for the selected period.

Referrers see their own referrals. Management and opndoor admins see the whole estate. Commission and fee figures are shown to management and opndoor admins only — see the Management supplement.

7. Applications, agencies & branches

The Applications screen lists referrals with search and status filters. The count beside a filter reflects the applications matching it.

The Agencies & branches screen shows the network you refer through. Click any figure to drill through to the applications behind it. You can also jump straight to a filtered list — for example, opening an agency or branch takes you to its applications.
