Sales & conversation guide

1. What opndoor is — in one line

opndoor is a professional guarantor. When a tenant can't provide their own guarantor, opndoor issues a Deed of Guarantee in favour of the property so the let can go ahead.

Say it precisely. It is a guarantor service, not insurance. opndoor stands behind the tenant's obligations under the tenancy; it does not insure the landlord against loss.

2. Who qualifies

The tenant should be someone who can afford the rent but doesn't fit a standard reference. Typical cases:

  • No UK-based guarantor available.
  • Self-employed, contract, or newly arrived in the UK.
  • Students and relocators.
  • Just under an affordability multiple, but demonstrably able to pay.

It is not a route for a tenant the agent believes cannot pay. The guarantee supports good tenants who fail referencing on form, not affordability that isn't there.

3. Talking to the agent

Lead with the outcome: a let that completes instead of falling through, and a reassured landlord.

Opener

"When a tenant's reference falls down but they can clearly afford it, opndoor can act as their guarantor so you don't lose the let — the landlord gets the same reassurance a personal guarantor gives."

Reassure the agent on their role: they remain the managing agent and the claim contact. Nothing about their relationship with the landlord changes.

4. Talking to the tenant

Keep it simple and honest: it's how they secure the home, what it costs, and what it doesn't change.

Framing

"opndoor will act as your guarantor so your application can go ahead. It's a one-off fee of one month's rent, and it doesn't change anything else about your tenancy — you pay your rent and look after the place exactly as normal."

Be clear the fee is one month's rent, paid once — there is no monthly charge. And if they have already signed their deed, nothing more is needed from them.

5. How claims work

  • The guarantee runs alongside the tenancy for its term.
  • If something is claimed under it, the referring agent is the point of contact — not the tenant directly, and not a separate insurer.
  • opndoor stands behind the tenant's obligations as guarantor; recovery from the tenant follows as it would with any guarantor.

6. Where the line is

Getting this right protects the tenant, the agent and opndoor. Hold these lines in every conversation:

Do say: professional guarantor · a Deed of Guarantee in favour of the property · the agent is the claim contact · one month's rent, once.

Don't say: "insurance" or "policy" · that opndoor is on the tenancy agreement · that it protects the tenant against their own liability · anything guaranteeing a specific claims outcome.

opndoor is not a party to the tenancy agreement and is not named on it. If you're ever unsure how to describe the service, defer to the wording in this guide and the Deed of Guarantee, and check with your opndoor partnerships contact.
