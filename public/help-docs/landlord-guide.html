A guarantor you can rely on

What the opndoor guarantee means for you and your property.

Your agent has proposed a tenant who is right for the property but cannot provide their own guarantor. opndoor acts as a professional guarantor so you can let with confidence.

The reassurance you get

opndoor provides a Deed of Guarantee in favour of the property. It gives you the cover a personal guarantor would — a professional party standing behind the tenant's obligations under the tenancy — without you having to rely on a friend or family member of the tenant.

Clear and simple. There is no cost to you as the landlord. The tenant pays a one-off guarantor fee of one month's rent. opndoor is not a party to your tenancy agreement and does not replace your agent — it stands behind the tenancy as guarantor.

How it fits your letting

  • Your agent refers the tenant and opndoor issues the Deed of Guarantee before the tenancy completes.
  • Your tenancy agreement is unchanged — the same AST, the same terms.
  • Your agent remains your managing contact and the claim contact for the guarantee.

Is this insurance?

No. It is a professional guarantor service. opndoor stands as guarantor for the tenant's obligations; it is not an insurance policy.

Who do I deal with if there's a problem?

Your letting agent, exactly as you do now. They are the point of contact for anything arising under the guarantee.

Does opndoor go on my tenancy agreement?

No. opndoor is not named on, or a party to, the tenancy agreement. The guarantee sits alongside it.
