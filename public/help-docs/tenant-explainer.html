Your guarantor, sorted

A simple explainer for tenants using the opndoor guarantor service.

You have found a home you want to rent, but you cannot provide a UK guarantor or fully pass referencing. opndoor can step in as your professional guarantor so the letting can go ahead.

What opndoor does

opndoor acts as a professional guarantor for your tenancy. We provide a Deed of Guarantee in favour of the property, which gives the landlord the same reassurance a personal guarantor would — so your application can proceed.

opndoor is not named on your tenancy agreement and is not your landlord or letting agent. This is a guarantor service, not insurance.

What it costs

One month's rent.

That is the one-off guarantor fee for the tenancy. There is nothing else to pay to opndoor, and no monthly charge.

How it works

  • Your letting agent sends your referral to opndoor through their portal.
  • You receive a secure link to pay the guarantor fee — one month's rent.
  • Once paid, opndoor issues the Deed of Guarantee and your tenancy can complete.
  • If you have already signed your deed, nothing more is needed from you.

Good to know. The guarantee runs alongside your tenancy. If anything is ever claimed under it, your letting agent is the point of contact — you deal with the same people you always have.

Your responsibilities don't change

You still pay your rent and look after the property exactly as your tenancy agreement sets out. The guarantee protects the landlord if something goes wrong; it does not change what you owe under your tenancy.

Questions about your tenancy should go to your letting agent. This explainer is a summary and does not form part of any tenancy agreement or the Deed of Guarantee. © opndoor.
